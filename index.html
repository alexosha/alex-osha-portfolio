Introduction

Nearly my whole life has been in pursuit of mastery -- whether of speed, strength, and endurance, or of the guitar, the written word, or more intellectual pursuits.

From my four years at Bowdoin College to my time in the United States Air Force, I have always sought to make my own abilities beyond expectations laid out by others for me. My philosophy in this is that the more I can do, the better I can leave an impact in this world.

Where I'm From

I'm unabashedly from the South. Born in Georgia, raised in North Carolina, I've spent nearly all my life in the humid, hot rolling hills and mountains of the South. I learned hospitality, respect, honor, and duty in my youth, which have stuck with me to this day, qualities I quickly recognize in fellow Southerners and often find lacking in those from other locales.

I have spent several years now in Maine, attending Bowdoin College for most of them, which I consider "the South of the North" in that the individuals here are hardy, respectful, independent, and honorable.

More About Me

What are your favorite hobbies?

In line with my dedication to perfection and mastery, I have spent much of my spare time throughout the years working on my capabilties as a human. Such things I consider vital to survival and flourishing: swimming, running, weight-lifting, shooting firearms, hiking, camping, musical instruments, visual and performing arts. These are all skills I have spent many an hour building up, and it does not hurt that I enjoy them.

What is your dream job?

Two of my passions combined: teaching and firearms. I have long dreamed of being a firearms instructor: teaching responsible citizens how to defend themselves, their families, and their communities.

What music have you been listening to lately?

I have always gravitated to more energetic, passionate, perhaps even dark music. For instance, my current favorites are Tool, Nine Inch Nails, and Alice in Chains.
